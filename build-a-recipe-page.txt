    <h1>Quick & easy tiramisu</h1>
    <p>Condensed milk is the secret to this super snappy Italian dessert. Coffee and chocolate are a classic combo, simply layer them up and enjoy</p>
    <h2>Ingredients</h2>
    <ul>
      <li>3 tsp instant coffee
granules</li>
      <li>3 tbsp coffee liqueur</li>
      <li>250g <a href="https://go.skimresources.com/?id=71026X1587439&isjs=1&jv=15.7.1&sref=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.bbcgoodfood.com%2Frecipes%2Fquick-easy-tiramisu&url=https%3A%2F%2Fgroceries.morrisons.com%2Fproducts%2Fmorrisons-mascarpone-250g%2F104667467&xs=1&xtz=-330&xuuid=46ded66ff2634e7c25e063d62321e481&xcust=bbcgoodfood-216608&xjsf=other_click__auxclick%20%5B2%5D">Morrisons Mascarpone</a></li>
      <li>85g <a href="https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/glossary/milk-glossary">condensed milk</a></li>
      <li>1 tsp <a href="https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/glossary/vanilla-glossary">vanilla extract</a></li>
      <li>4-6 sponge fingers</li>
      <li>1 tbsp cocoa powder</li>
    </ul>
    <h2>Instructions</h2>
    <ol>
      <li>Mix the coffee granules with 2 tbsp boiling water in a large jug and stir to combine. Add the coffee liqueur and 75ml cold water. Pour into a shallow dish and set aside.</li>
      <li>Make the cream layer by beating the Morrisons Mascarpone, condensed milk and vanilla extract with an electric whisk until thick and smooth.</li>
      <li>Break the sponge fingers into two or three pieces and soak in the coffee mixture for a few secs. Put a few bits of the sponge in the bottom of two wine or sundae glasses and top with the cream.</li>
      <li>Sift over the cocoa and chill for at least 1 hr before serving.</li>
      <img src="https://atsloanestable.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/tiramisu3.jpg" alt="TIRAMISU">
     </ol>
      <h2>Recipe Tips</h2>
      <h3>USES FOR SPONGE FINGERS</h3>
      <p>Sponge fingers make an ideal base for trifles too. Use leftovers in a super-quick version: pour a little syrup from any canned fruit over the fingers to soak them, then top with cream and the fruit from the can.</p>
      <h3>Recipe from Good Food magazine, February 2016</h3>
